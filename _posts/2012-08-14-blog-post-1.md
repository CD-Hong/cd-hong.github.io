## Overview

This post documents the process of updating **Bond** and **Equity** data from **LSEG Datastream**, including consistency checks, coverage expansion, and automation via **VBA scripting**.

---

## 1. Checking Data Consistency

To ensure data continuity, I re-downloaded the data for the overlapping period between the previous and new dataset: **January to July 2023**.

Upon comparison, I observed notable differences in the monthly values:

- While some differences are minor and may reflect regular updates,
- Others show **non-trivial deviations**, raising concerns about structural or definitional changes.

👉 I recommend validating any significant gaps or breaks in time-series before integration.

---

## 2. Coverage Expansion Summary

The updated data significantly expands coverage for both bond and equity instruments.

| Category        | Before    | After     |
|----------------|-----------|-----------|
| Bond Static     | 280,371   | 460,050   |
| Equity Static   | 84,167    | 91,438    |

This enhancement may affect any downstream filters, aggregation logic, or modeling processes.

---

## 3. VBA Automation

To streamline the data download process from LSEG Datastream, I used the sample Excel macro:

- **File**: `Equity_Monthly_Q.xlsm`
- **Macro Used**: `ProcessRowsOneByOne()`

This macro enables efficient row-by-row querying of long ticker lists using pre-configured request templates.

📂 A brief instruction file and the macro script have been saved in the `/description` folder for future reference.

---

## 4. Notes for Further Validation

- Be cautious of **data revisions** in Datastream, especially for computed fields (e.g., returns, yields).
- Always check variable definitions and calculation bases across time if discrepancies persist.

---

## Summary

Updating data from LSEG Datastream is not just about pulling new numbers. It requires:

- **Careful consistency checks** for continuity,
- **Understanding of coverage expansion**, and
- **Automation tools** to manage large-scale downloads efficiently.
