This study conducts a dynamic shift-share analysis to examine the evolution of Incheon’s industrial structure over the past decades. The report diagnoses key structural changes, identifies growth and decline sectors, and evaluates the implications for regional competitiveness. It concludes with targeted policy recommendations to promote balanced and sustainable economic development in Incheon.

Prior to the COVID-19 pandemic, Incheon experienced a marked shift toward a service-oriented economy, with the share of services in both industrial structure and employment increasing significantly, driven by regional competitive advantages. However, during the pandemic, service sector competitiveness—particularly in transportation and warehousing—declined sharply, while manufacturing and construction showed relatively resilient performance. Incheon needs to enhance its industrial structure and promote digital transformation by fostering high-productivity, knowledge-based services, such as the MICE and medical tourism industries, and improving competitiveness in manufacturing and logistics through smart factories and smart logistics centers.
