Using a dynamic shift–share framework, this study examines Incheon’s transition toward a service oriented economy. Regional competitiveness initially boosted the share of services in both value added and employment. However, during the pandemic this trend reversed. Transportation and warehousing weakened service-sector competitiveness, while manufacturing and construction remained relatively resilient. Based on these findings, we propose a pragmatic path for the regional economy.
