I examine whether private bankers, specialized financial professionals employed by commercial banks to serve high-net-worth clients, create value for their customers. Contrary to the conventional view that financial experts often exploit retail investors, I find that South Korean private bankers improve customer investment outcomes. Specifically, private bankers reduce clients’ disposition effect by 24% and guide them toward products with more customer-friendly fee structures, generating an average annualized benefit of approximately 8 basis points. These positive effects dissipate when bankers are scheduled for reassignment to another branch or are approaching retirement, suggesting that value creation is closely tied to incentive compatibility and the expected continuation of the client–banker relationship. Overall, the findings indicate that, under the right incentive structure, specialized financial intermediaries can deliver genuine value to retail investors rather than extract rents from them.
